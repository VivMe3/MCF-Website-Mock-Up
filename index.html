About

Maryland Christian Fellowship (MCF) - Students is a student group at the University of Maryland, College Park. We strive to be a place where you can explore and share your faith in a safe and encouraging environment. We're a Christian group that's not just for Chrstians...Crazy!...Right?


Whether you are a life-long Christian or you’ve never set foot into a church-like event before, you are welcome to join us in a variety of ways including the Sunday Worship Service, weekly growth groups (MCF's version of small groups), service projects from our own neighborhoods to international trips, and plenty of opportunities for straight up fun. Don’t just learn about God, experience life with Him.

Meetings

Sunday Service

11:30AM - 12:25PM
2203 Art Sociology

Our Sunday services aim to be an opportunity for Christians and non-Christians to see how God affects real, everyday life. What to expect when you first visit - Our band kicks the service off with a time to worship and prepare our hearts to learn, then we have a contemporary and relevant teaching, and we conclude with time for informal fun and fellowship. Come as you are. Dress is casual.

Growth Groups

The goal of Growth Groups is to help members grow deeper in their relationship with God in a more personal and interactive environment than Sunday Service can provide. Groups get together each week to learn about the Bible and to have fun together. It’s a great way to meet new people while exploring your faith. There are four great reasons to join a Growth Group:

  • Weekly pick-me-up that doesn’t eat up your schedule
  • Prayer from others helps you get through the tough times
  • Meet new friends in the area
  • Have fun with other people going through the same things you are

Statement of Faith


The sole basis of our beliefs is the Bible, the 66 books of the Old and New Testaments. It was uniquely, verbally and fully inspired by the Holy Spirit and was written without error in the original manuscripts. It is the supreme and final authority in faith and life in every age.

Psalms 12:6, 119:105, 160; Proverbs 30; 2 Timothy 1:13, 3:16; 2 Peter 1:20-21

There is but one God, infinite in power, wisdom, justice, goodness and love, the Creator of the universe, eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son and Holy Spirit, each of whom possesses all the attributes of Deity and the characteristics of personality.

Genesis 1:1, 26, 27, 3:22; Psalm 90:2; Matthew 28:19; 2 Corinthians 13:14; 1 Peter 1:2

God created man in His own image, and man, as he was originally created, was innocent before God. But man chose to sin by disobeying God and therefore was alienated from his Creator and came under divine condemnation. Thus all human beings are born with a corrupted nature and without spiritual life, and are totally incapable of pleasing God in and of themselves.

Genesis 1:27; Psalms 8:3-6; Isaiah 53:6 59:1-2; Romans 3:23

God the Father, by His own choice and out of love for sinful men and women, sent Jesus Christ was conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of a virgin. He is God in the flesh, both true God and true man.
Jesus Christ lived a sinless life and voluntarily suffered and died as our substitute to pay the penalty for our sins, thus satisfying God’s justice and accomplishing salvation for all who trust in Him alone. He rose from the dead in the same body, though glorified, in which He lived and died. He bodily ascended into heaven and sat down at the right hand of God the Father, where He, the only mediator between God and man, makes intercession for His own. He will return to earth personally, visibly and bodily to judge all men and establish His Kingdom.

Isaiah 9:6; Matthew 1:22-23; John 1:1-5, 14:10-30; Acts 1:9-11; Romans 1:3-4; 1 Corinthians 15:3-4; 2 Timothy 6:14-15; Titus 2:13; Hebrews 4:14-15

Men and women are freed from the penalty for their sins not as a result, in whole or in part, of their own works, goodness or religious ceremony, but by the undeserved favor of God alone. God declares righteous all who put their faith in Christ alone for their salvation.

John 1:12, 14:6; Romans 5:1, 6:23; Galatians 3:26; Ephesians 2:8-9; Titus 3:5

The Holy Spirit has come into the world to reveal and glorify Christ, to convict men and women of their sins and to impart new life to all who place their faith in Christ. He indwells believers from the moment of spiritual birth, seals them until the day of redemption and empowers them to live a life pleasing to God.

John 14:16-17, 16:7-13; Acts 1:8; 2 Corinthians 2:12, 3:16-17; Galatians 5:25; Ephesians 1:13, 5:18

All who are born again of the Spirit can be fully assured of eternal life from the very moment they put their faith in Christ. This assurance is not based upon any kind of human merit, but upon the fact of God’s election, upon Christ’s complete payment for sins and upon the indwelling of the Holy Spirit, who is the deposit guaranteeing their inheritance.

John 10:29; 2 Timothy 1:12; Hebrews 7:25, 10:10-14; 1 Peter 1:3-5

At physical death, the believer enters immediately into eternal, conscious fellowship with the Lord and awaits the resurrection of his or her own body to everlasting glory and blessing. At physical death, the unbeliever enters immediately into eternal, conscious separation from the Lord and awaits the resurrection of his or her body to everlasting suffering, judgment and condemnation.

John 3:16; Romans 6:23; 1 John 2:25, 5:11-13; Revelation 20:15

Jesus Christ has instructed those who believe in Him to be baptized in water as a symbol of their new birth in Christ. Baptism should be by immersion and is only for those who have personally believed in Christ. The Lord also instituted the Lord’s Supper as a remembrance of His suffering, death and has any merit in helping a person obtain eternal life.

Matthew 26:26-30, 28:18-20; Mark 1:9-10; 1 Corinthians 11:17-34; Colossians 2:12

All true believers make up the church worldwide and should assemble together in local churches – for worship, prayer, fellowship, and teaching to become conformed to the image of Christ and to become equipped to carry out the “Great Commission” that Christ gave His followers in Matthew 28:18-20.

Matthew 16:18, 28:18-20; Romans 12:4-5; Galatians 6:10; Ephesians 2:19; Hebrews 2:10-12; 1 Peter 3:8, 4:17
